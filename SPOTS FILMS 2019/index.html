Peter Siaggas

Director Reel

A member of the Screen Actor’s Guild since the age of 12, Peter was an international Coca-Cola kid, featured on every Coke vending machine around the world. He appeared in over 75 commercials, TV shows and movies and was the principal lead in the longest running McDonalds commercial to date. Born in Milwaukee and raised in Reno, Siaggas became a scratch golfer at age 16. He competed in both national and international golf and turned down several golf scholarships to pursue film production. His love for the industry propelled him into a film production major. He served as vice president at Fini Non-Linear Post Production for several years, began directing full-time in 2000, and started his own production company, Spots Films, in 2005. Siaggas currently resides in Atlanta, GA.

Brad Beenders

Director Reel

Brad Beenders is a director/DP who specializes in commercials and documentaries. Brad’s directing style quickly emerged after borrowing a 16mm Bolex camera and running around southern California for a few years. His eye for capturing intimate and unique imagery are evident in his work, which emphasizes realism and honesty with visual impact. Brad’s Midwestern roots continue to influence and inspire him and helps drive his passion for creating engaging cinematic commercials and stories.

Ben Van Hook

Director Reel

Born and raised in Kentucky, Ben started his career at The Louisville Courier-Journal. Relocating to Florida in 1990 he started working for Sports Illustrated, The New York Times Magazine, Fortune, and a host of other national publications. In 1999 Ben was awarded the prestigious Robert F. Kennedy Memorial Award in International Photojournalism for a LIFE magazine story. In 2001 he co-directed his first feature length documentary which was acquired by SHOWTIME. His film credits include Lifetime TV, FOX, and National Geographic Explorer. Ben has transitioned to directing commerical work over the past 10 years, and has a one-of-a-kind ability to create “Real Life” on film.

Steve Andrich

Director Reel

Regarded as one of the world’s best sports and extreme-action cinematographers, Steve Andrich’s style has become synonymous with excellence inside the sports arena and out. He has been honored with numerous awards, including six National Emmys and a Cannes Silver Lion for transforming spontaneous, often chaotic motion into indelible storytelling images. From 1995 – 2004, Andrich served as vice president of cinematography for NFL FILMS. In this role, Andrich expanded the studio’s legacy as legendary cinéma vérité documentarians, while bringing the studio’s 70 cinematographers into a new era of filmmaking. As a Director, Steve’s work includes National spots for E-Trade, Olive Garden, and MLB16 The Show. His spot, “Piece by Piece”’ a PSA for the ALS Society won the National Addy and his campaign to stop Teen Age drunk Driving, “All Apologies”, won multiple Addy’s including Best of Show at the Philadelphia Addy Award show.

Spots is a full-service film production company based in Atlanta, Georgia that specializes in shooting comedy and branded content as well as table top and long-form television.
